Organizational Overview 
Green Ahom Federation continued its humanitarian and developmental journey during the Financial Year 
2024–25 by implementing multiple health, nutrition, environmental, women empowerment, rural 
development, animal welfare, and community support programmes across various districts of Assam. 
The organization worked extensively among vulnerable populations including tuberculosis patients, 
economically weaker households, women, children, elderly citizens, rural communities, and stray animals. 
Through grassroots participation, volunteer support, and community engagement, the organization 
successfully reached thousands of beneficiaries across Cachar, Hailakandi, Karimganj, Barpeta, Nalbari, 
Nagaon, and NC Hills districts. 
The year reflected the organization’s strong commitment towards sustainable development, healthcare 
accessibility, social welfare, and inclusive community empowerment.

Message from the Director 
It gives me immense pleasure to present the Annual Report of Green Ahom Federation for the Financial 
Year 2024–25. This year has been a remarkable journey of service, commitment, compassion, and collective 
effort towards the welfare and empowerment of vulnerable communities across Assam. 
During the year, our organization continued to work tirelessly in the areas of healthcare support, nutritional 
assistance, women empowerment, environmental sustainability, rural infrastructure development, 
humanitarian relief, and animal welfare. Through various impactful programmes, we were able to extend our 
support to economically weaker sections, tuberculosis patients, women, children, elderly persons, rural 
communities, and stray animals across multiple districts. 
One of the major highlights of this year was the successful implementation of nutritional food packet 
distribution programmes for tuberculosis patients in Patharkandi Block of Karimganj District. The initiative 
significantly supported patients undergoing treatment by improving their nutritional intake and encouraging 
better recovery outcomes. 
Our healthcare interventions also included eye camps, spectacle distribution, and cataract surgery support 
programmes conducted in Hailakandi District. These programmes brought meaningful changes in the lives 
of many elderly and underprivileged individuals by restoring vision and improving quality of life. 
Women empowerment remained one of our strongest priorities during the year. Skill development and 
handicraft training programmes conducted across Cachar, Hailakandi, Nagaon, NC Hills, Algapur, and 
Lakhipur areas helped women and youth gain vocational skills, improve confidence, and create livelihood 
opportunities for sustainable economic development. 
Environmental conservation initiatives through plantation drives in schools and government office premises 
encouraged community participation and awareness regarding ecological sustainability and climate 
responsibility. 
The organization also undertook humanitarian food distribution programmes for eliminating hunger among 
vulnerable populations across several districts of Assam. These initiatives provided essential relief and 
nutritional support to economically weaker families and daily wage earners facing hardship. 
In addition, our animal welfare programmes focusing on feeding and medical support for stray and 
vulnerable animals reflected our commitment towards compassion and humane responsibility towards all 
living beings. 
The construction of community centres in Barpeta, Cachar, and Hailakandi districts further strengthened 
community infrastructure and created opportunities for social, educational, and developmental activities at 
the grassroots level. 
These achievements would not have been possible without the sincere dedication of our volunteers, staff 
members, healthcare professionals, trainers, community leaders, supporters, and partner organizations. I 
extend my heartfelt gratitude to every individual and institution who contributed towards the successful 
implementation of our programmes during the year. As we move forward, Green Ahom Federation remains 
committed towards building an inclusive, healthy, sustainable, and empowered society through continued 
humanitarian service and community development initiatives. 
Together, we can continue creating meaningful and lasting social impact. 
With Warm Regards, 
Director

1. Nutritional Food Packets Distribution to TB Patients 
Patharkandi Block, Karimganj District 
Budget: ₹14,45,000 
Programme Overview 
Tuberculosis patients from economically disadvantaged 
backgrounds often suffer from severe nutritional 
deficiencies, directly affecting their immunity and 
recovery process. Recognizing the importance of 
nutritional support during treatment, Green Ahom 
Federation implemented a nutritional food packet 
distribution programme in Patharkandi Block of 
Karimganj District. 
The programme aimed to improve the nutritional status of TB patients and reduce the economic burden 
faced by vulnerable households during prolonged medical treatment. 
Activities Conducted 
 Identification of TB patients through 
community coordination.  
 Distribution of nutritional food 
packets.  
 Awareness sessions regarding 
nutrition and TB care.  
 Follow-up interaction with 
beneficiaries and families.  
Nutritional Materials Distributed 
The packets included: 
 Rice  
 Pulses  
 Cooking oil  
 Protein supplements  
 Nutritious food products  
 Essential dietary materials  
Impact Generated 
The intervention generated significant positive 
outcomes: 
 Improved nutritional support for TB 
patients.  
 Better treatment adherence and recovery 
support.  
 Reduction in household financial stress.  
 Enhanced awareness regarding healthcare and nutrition.  
 Increased emotional support and community confidence.

2. Eye Camp – Spectacles Distribution for Refractive Error Programme 
Budget: ₹2,69,750 
Programme Overview 
Uncorrected refractive errors remain a major 
challenge among rural populations where 
access to eye care services is limited. Poor 
vision affects education, livelihood, mobility, 
and quality of life. 
To address this issue, Green Ahom Federation 
organized eye screening camps and spectacle 
distribution programmes in Lala Block of 
Hailakandi District. 
Activities Conducted 
 Vision testing and eye examinations.  
 Identification of refractive errors.  
 Spectacle prescription and distribution.  
 Awareness regarding preventive eye 
care.  
Impact Generated 
 Improved eyesight among 
beneficiaries.  
 Better educational performance among 
students.  
 Increased mobility and independence among elderly persons.  
 Improved work productivity among daily wage earners.  
 Enhanced awareness regarding eye health.

3. Eye Camp – Cataract Surgery Support Programme By ONGC Silchar CSR 
Lala Block, South Hailakandi Block & 
Algapur Block 
Budget: ₹1,59,590

elderly rural populations. Due to financial

Green Ahom Federation implemented cataract

Activities Conducted 
 Free eye screening camps.  
 Identification of cataract patients.  
 Referral and surgery support.  
 Community awareness regarding eye care.  
Impact Generated 
 Restoration of vision among elderly 
beneficiaries.  
 Improved confidence and mobility.  
 Reduction in avoidable blindness.  
 Better healthcare awareness among rural 
populations.
