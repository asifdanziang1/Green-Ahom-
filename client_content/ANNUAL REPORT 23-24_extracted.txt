About the Organization 
Introduction 
Green Ahom Federation is a non-profit voluntary organization dedicated to social development, humanitarian 
assistance, public health promotion, women and child welfare, environmental sustainability, and community 
empowerment across Assam and the Barak Valley region. 
During the Financial Year 2023–2024, the organization intensified its grassroots interventions in health, 
nutrition, food security, and humanitarian relief through collaborative initiatives with government departments, 
healthcare stakeholders, local communities, and corporate social responsibility (CSR) partners. 
The organization continued to work among vulnerable and marginalized populations including tuberculosis 
patients, pregnant women, children, daily wage earners, elderly people, and economically weaker households. 
Through community-based outreach and participatory implementation methods, the organization successfully 
carried out multiple impactful welfare programmes across Cachar, Hailakandi, and Karimganj districts of 
Assam. 
Vision 
To build a healthy, equitable, sustainable, and empowered society where every individual has access to dignity, 
nutrition, healthcare, livelihood opportunities, and social justice. 
Mission 
 To support vulnerable communities through humanitarian and developmental interventions.  
 To strengthen public health and nutrition awareness among marginalized populations.  
 To promote community participation and social inclusion.  
 To implement sustainable welfare programmes through partnerships and collaborative action.  
 To improve the quality of life of disadvantaged populations through health, nutrition, education, and 
empowerment initiatives.

Message from the Director 
The Financial Year 2023 –2024 has been a year of dedicated service, compassion, and meaningful outreach for 
Green Ahom Federation. Our organization remained deeply committed to serving vulnerable populations across 
the Barak Valley region through targeted n utrition support, healthcare services, eye care interventions, and 
humanitarian assistance. 
One of the major achievements of this year was the successful implementation of the Nutritional Food Packet 
Distribution Programme for 935 tuberculosis patients in Cachar and Hailakandi districts with the support of 
ONGC Silchar. This initiative significantly contributed toward improving nutritional support among 
economically disadvantaged TB patients, thereby supporting treatment adherence and recovery. 
We also orga nized a large -scale eye screening and spectacle distribution programme in Hailakandi district 
benefitting around 500 individuals suffering from refractive errors and cataract -related complications. The 
intervention improved awareness regarding eye health a nd facilitated access to essential ophthalmic care 
services among rural communities. 
In addition, the organization extended support to daily wage earners through dry ration distribution during 
Independence Day celebrations and implemented a maternal and child nutrition programme in the Luairpoa area 
of Karimganj district focusing on pregnant women and children below three years of age. 
These achievements were possible because of the support of our donors, volunteers, health professionals, 
community workers,  local stakeholders, and beneficiaries. We remain committed to continuing our efforts 
toward inclusive and sustainable community development.

Major Activities Conducted During FY 2023–2024 
 
1. Nutritional Food Packet Distribution to 935 TB Patients  
Supported by ONGC Silchar 
Budget: Rs. 14,85,000 
Programme Background 
Tuberculosis remains one of the major public 
health concerns among economically weaker 
communities. Poor nutritional status significantly 
affects treatment adherence, immunity levels, and 
recovery rates among TB patients. Understanding 
the urgent need for nutritional intervention, Green 
Ahom Federation implemented a comprehensive 
Nutritional Food Packe t Distribution Programme 
for tuberculosis patients in Cachar and Hailakandi 
districts. 
The programme was implemented with the 
valuable CSR support of Oil and Natural Gas 
Corporation (ONGC Silchar). 
Objectives of the Programme 
 To provide nutritional support to economically vulnerable TB patients.  
 To improve immunity and 
treatment recovery among 
patients.  
 To encourage regular 
treatment adherence.  
 To reduce social vulnerability 
among affected families.  
 To create awareness regarding 
nutrition and tuberculosis 
management.  
Coverage Area  
 Cachar District  
 Hailakandi District  
Beneficiaries 
 935 registered tuberculosis patients from economically weaker backgrounds.  


--- PAGE 6 ---
Activities Conducted 
Identification of Beneficiaries 
Beneficiaries were identified in coordination with 
local health authorities, TB treatment centres, 
community health workers, and district-level

Nutritious food packets containing essential

 Hygiene and sanitation practices.  
 Medication adherence.  
 Prevention of disease transmission.  
 Psychological and emotional support.  
Community Outreach 
Field volunteers and healthcare facilitators visited 
remote and vulnerable localities to ensure last-mile 
delivery and beneficiary support. 
Programme Impact 
 Improved nutritional intake among TB 
patients.  
 Enhanced treatment compliance and 
patient morale.  
 Reduced financial burden on vulnerable 
families.  
 Increased awareness regarding tuberculosis 
management.  
 Strengthened community-healthcare 
coordination.  
 Encouraged social inclusion and reduction of stigma associated with TB.

2. Eye Camp, Spectacle Distribution & Cataract Identification Programme  
Hailakandi District 
Budget: Rs. 11,17,500 
Programme Background 
Access to affordable eye care remains limited in many rural and underserved areas of Assam. Poor vision, 
untreated refractive errors, and cataract -related complications often lead to reduced productivity, educational 
difficulties, and poor quality of life. 
To address these challenges, Green Ahom Federation organized a comprehensive Eye  Screening and Eye Care 
Support Programme in Hailakandi district. 
Objectives  
 To provide free eye screening services to 
vulnerable communities.  
 To identify refractive errors and cataract cases.  
 To distribute spectacles among needy individuals.  
 To facilitate referrals for cataract surgeries.  
 To promote awareness regarding eye health and 
preventive care.  
Beneficiaries 
 Approximately 500 individuals screened and supported.  
Activities Conducted 
Free Eye Screening Camp  
A comprehensive eye examination camp was organized with the 
support of qualified ophthalmologists, optometrists, and 
healthcare volunteers. 
Identification of Refractive Errors 
Patients suffering from vision-related difficulties were screened and diagnosed for refractive errors. 
Spectacle Distribution 
Corrective spectacles were distributed free of cost among economically weaker individuals requiring visual 
support. 
Cataract Identification & Referral

 Nutrition and eye health  
 Early identification of cataract symptoms

be a major developmental concern in rural

women and young children require adequate 
nutrition to ensure healthy growth, safe 
pregnancy outcomes, and improved 
immunity.

Karimganj district.

children below three years.  
 To support healthy pregnancy outcomes.

 Lactating mothers  
 Children below three years of age

supportive food materials were distributed among beneficiaries.

Health educators conducted sessions on:
